Symptoms - Menopause

Changes to your periods

The first sign of the menopause is usually a change in the normal pattern of your periods. They may get lighter or heavier, and happen less often.

Eventually, you'll stop having periods altogether..

Symptoms usually start months or years before your periods stop. This is called the perimenopause.

Common symptoms of perimenopause and menopause

Not everyone gets symptoms, but if you do they can have a big impact on your daily life..

Your symptoms may be worse if your menopause comes on suddenly, for example after surgery to remove your ovaries.

Mental health symptoms

Common mental health symptoms of menopause and perimenopause include:

  • hanges to your mood, like low mood, anxiety, mood swings and low self-esteem
  • problems with memory or concentration (brain fog)

Physical symptoms

Common physical symptoms of menopause and perimenopause include:

  • hot flushes, when you have short, sudden feelings of heat or cold, usually in your face, neck and chest. They can make you sweaty or dizzy
  • night sweats
  • difficulty sleeping, which may be a result of the night sweats and make you feel tired and irritable during the day
  • palpitations, when your heartbeats suddenly become more noticeable
  • headaches and migraines that are worse than usual
  • muscle aches and joint pains
  • changing body shape and weight gain
  • skin changes including dry and itchy skin
  • reduced sex drive (libido)
  • vaginal dryness and pain, itching or discomfort during sex
  • recurrent urinary tract infections (UTIs)

Non-urgent advice: Get advice from a GP if:

  • you think you have perimenopause or menopause symptoms and want to know more about treatment
  • perimenopause or menopause symptoms are a problem
  • you're under 45 and think you have perimenopause or menopause symptoms

A doctor or nurse at your GP surgery will be able to give you information about self-care and treatments.

How long symptoms last

Menopause is different for everyone. You may have a number of symptoms or none.

Symptoms can last for months or years, and can change with time.

For example, hot flushes and night sweats may improve, and then you may develop low mood and anxiety.

Important: Important

Getting advice early can help reduce the impact symptoms of perimenopause and menopause have on your health, relationships and work.
