Overview - Menopause

Contents

  1. Overview
  2. Symptoms
  3. Things you can do
  4. Treatments
  5. Further help and support
  • Menopause is when your periods stop due to lower hormone levels. This usually happens between the ages of 40 and 55.
  • It can sometimes happen earlier. Reasons for this can include surgery to remove the ovaries (oophorectomy) or the uterus (hysterectomy), cancer treatments like chemotherapy, or a genetic reason. Sometimes the reason is unknown.
  • Menopause can cause symptoms like anxiety, mood swings, brain fog, hot flushes and irregular periods. These symptoms can start years before your periods stop and carry on afterwards.
  • When you have symptoms and your periods have not stopped, this is called the perimenopause. You reach menopause when you have not had a period for 12 months.
  • Menopause symptoms can have a big impact on your life, including relationships and work.
  • There are things you can do to help with symptoms. There are also medicines that can replace the missing hormones and relieve your symptoms.
